10A NCAC 27G .0207 EMERGENCY PLANS
AND SUPPLIES

(a) Awritten fire plan for each facility and
area-wide disaster plan shall be developed and
shall be approved by the appropriate local
authority.

(b) The plan shall be made available to all staff
and evacuation procedures and routes shall be
posted in the facility.

(c) Fire and disaster drills in a 24-hour facility
shall be held at least quarterly and shall be
repeated for each shift. Drills shall be conducted
under conditions that simulate fire emergencies.
(d) Each facility shall have basic first aid supplies
accessible for use.

10A NCAC 27G .0304 FACILITY DESIGN AND
EQUIPMENT

(b) Safety: Each facility shall be designed,
constructed and equipped in a manner that
ensures the physical safety of clients, staff and
visitors.

(4) In areas of the facility where clients are
exposed to hot water, the temperature of the
water shall be maintained between 100-116
degrees Fahrenheit.

This Rule is not met as evidenced by:

Based on observation and interviews the facility
failed to maintain the facility water temperature
between 100-116 degrees Fahrenheit. The
findings are:

Observation of the facility on 2/4/20 at
approximately 11:15 AM revealed :

-The kitchen sink water temperature was 119
degrees Fahrenheit.

-Bathroom water temperature was 119 degrees
Fahrenheit.

Observation of the facility on 2/4/20 at
approximately 2:00 PM revealed:

-The kitchen sink water temperature was 119
degrees Fahrenheit.

-Bathroom water temperature was 119 degrees
Fahrenheit.

Observation on 2/4/20 of staff measuring water
temperature revealed:
-Staff was using a food thermometer.

Interview on 2/4/20 with the Home Manager
revealed:

-She did not realize the water temperature was
over 116 degrees.
