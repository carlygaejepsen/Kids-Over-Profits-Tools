Personnel Records – Licenses, Certifications, Training:
There was a total of fourteen personnel records reviewed, four (29%) professional and ten (26%)
paraprofessionals. There were no deficiencies noted during the personnel record review.
Observations:
At the conclusion of the onsite inspection, AFMC staff reviewed the final document request form with the Chief
Executive Officer and the Director of Risk Management at the completion of the on-site Inspection of Care and
the provider signed the acknowledgement of manual requirements that were not made available in the provider’s
policy and procedures.
Clinical Summary
As a part of the Quality-of-Care survey of the IOC, an active Fee for Service (FFS) Medicaid client list was
requested, client and/or guardian interviews were conducted, and a clinical record review was completed. The
following is a summary of findings and noted deficiencies.
Client/Guardian Interviews:
There was no active FFS Medicaid clients currently admitted at the time of IOC. Therefore, no client interviews
were conducted.
Program Activity/Service Milieu Observation:
Staff and clients were observed throughout the facility in a free time group activity in the outside courtyard, in the
classroom setting on the school unit, and in the cafeteria eating lunch. Clients were engaged in schoolwork and
activities. Staff were calmly interacting with clients and providing a therapeutic environment that was conducive
for learning and treatment therapies. All areas with clients were noted to be staffed adequately and according to
the facility’s staffing ratio policy.
Medication Pass:
No active FFS Medicaid clients received medications during a medication pass while AFMC staff were onsite.
Due to the observation of non-Medicaid clients not being compliant with the HIPAA minimal necessary rule, no
medication pass was observed. The AFMC RN visited with the Director of Nursing who was able to show the
AFMC RN the facility policies and procedures regarding medication administration,
count/reconciliation/handling of controlled substances, and medication errors/discrepancies.
The Director of Nursing was able to verbalize the step-by-step process of medication delivery including the
demonstration of the five rights of medication administration, documentation of medication delivery,
verification of client taking medication including mouth/cheek checks, and how clients are educated about each
medication. The facility nurse also verbalized the process utilized when a client refuses medication. A tour of
the medication room was completed. The medication room was found to be extremely clean and well organized.
As previously stated in the above facility tour, the facility has changed outside pharmacy companies to help with
the medication delivery system to increase safety in medication administration to the clients. The facility has
also purchased a brand-new medication cart. AFMC staff noted that the medication keys were hanging in the
lock of the medication cart and were not being carried on the medication nurse which is per facility policy. The
controlled substances are to remain double locked. The medication room door was locked, which is the first lock
but the medication cart which houses the controlled substance was not secured as the second lock. The Director
of Nursing took the keys to secure them until the keys could be given back to the medication nurse.
