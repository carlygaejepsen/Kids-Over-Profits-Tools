Licensing received a provider reported incident on 4/21/2025 for ELS Case #029113.
Licensing Specialist Horton met with Mr. Cole Mitchell at the office on Merrill Drive to review video footage
for this provider reported incident.
Licensing Specialist observed via video footage a ratio of 3:11. The residents were in a classroom. Licensing
observed Resident 1 sitting at a desk in the classroom. Licensing observed other residents walking and
moving around the classroom during the video footage review.
There was no audible/sound for this video footage. Licensing observed Resident 2 sitting in the back of the
classroom. Resident 2 got up and walked towards the front of the classroom. It appeared that another resident
in the classroom may have said something to Resident 2 that frustrated her. Resident 2 walked to the front of
the classroom where there was a staff member.
It appeared Resident 2 talked to the staff member. After talking to the staff member, Resident 2 walked
around in the classroom as if she was looking for someone or frustrated. Two staff members noticed Resident
2 and approached her. It appeared that the staff members had some concerns regarding Resident 2’s
movement in classroom.
After the staff members spoke with Resident 2, she followed one staff member to his desk and stood by him
where she later threw a desk. Staff members approached Resident 2 and it appeared that the staff members
used deescalating techniques to keep her from throwing more items or disrespecting staff or peers. After the
staff members spoke with Resident 2; she did not display any disruptive behavior at that time.
It appeared that Resident 2 threw an item at a male resident in the classroom. The male resident threw the item
back at Resident 2. Resident 2 then threw what appeared to be a calculator towards the male resident. It
appeared that the calculator missed the male resident. Resident 2 threw a second calculator at the male
resident. The male resident ducked, and the second calculator hit Resident 1 in the back of her head. Staff
responded immediately to check on Resident 1.
While Resident 2 was throwing things, staff members were monitoring Resident 2 and the other residents to
ensure everyone was safe. The residents were not removed from the classroom. However, the staff members
had quick responses to keep order in the classroom.
