Narrative - Dorris, Edward - 10/6/2024 4:10:37 AM (Initial)

On 10/05/2024 | was dispatched to 211 Church Street for a 13 year old juvenile overdose from taking pills. |
arrived at the address with first responders. After a couple minutes waiting on someone to let us in, an employee
came out from the back side door and let us in. There was already 1-2 first responders on scene and inside the
building and stated that the juvenile was alert and responsive as | was walking down the hallway. After the info was
given, | then turned back to one of the doors to allow the remaining first responders to get in. As | was holding the
door open, | was told to bring a Narcan to the juvenile. | then ran to where to the juvenile was laying and
administered one 4mg dosage of Narcan to the juvenile. The juvenile then opened his eyes and began talking to
myself and Chief Parrish. Myself and Chief Parrish continued talking to the juvenile until EMS arrived on scene and
placed the juvenile onto the stretcher and took him to the ambulance. A female employee followed the ambulance to
the hospital.

Later approximately 4 hours later | went back to the address to get a statement from the female employee
that went to the hospital with the juvenile along with a statement from the employee that seen the juvenile in the act.
The female employee stated that the other employee that seen the incident occur was gone on break and would be
back in roughly an hour. | left two witness statement forms with the female and advised her that | would come back to
pick them up after the other employee returned from break. | left my phone number with the employee to give me a
call when the statements were finished. | have not received a phone call at this time (4:21 AM , 10/06/2024).

An attempt to obtain the witness statements will be made on 10/06/2024 when | return to shift. Nothing
further at this time.
